MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into and made effective as of October 5, 2022 (the "Effective Date"), by and between Saltmarsh Biotech, Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 410 Tidewater Avenue, Wilmington, Delaware 19801 ("Saltmarsh"), and Weir Instruments LLC, a Delaware limited liability company with its principal place of business at 88 Millrace Court, Newark, Delaware 19711 ("Weir"). Saltmarsh and Weir are each a "Party" and together the "Parties." The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship relating to analytical instrumentation for bioprocess monitoring (the "Purpose"), in connection with which each may disclose confidential information to the other.

1. DEFINITIONS.

1.1 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information, in any form or medium, disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party") in connection with the Purpose, whether before or after the Effective Date, including without limitation technical data, trade secrets, know-how, research plans, product designs, assay protocols, instrument specifications, software, business plans, financial information, customer and supplier lists, and pricing, in each case whether or not marked or otherwise identified as confidential, provided that a reasonable person would understand such information to be confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.

1.2 "Representatives" means, with respect to a Party, such Party's directors, officers, employees, attorneys, accountants, and financial advisors.

2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS. The Receiving Party shall (a) hold the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party; (c) use such Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose; and (d) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care.

3. EXCLUSIONS. The obligations set forth in Section 2 shall not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent written evidence: (a) is or becomes generally known to the public through no fault of or breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully known to the Receiving Party, free of any obligation of confidentiality, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party; (c) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation owed to the Disclosing Party; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

4. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE. If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or valid court or governmental order to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so without breaching Section 2, provided that it (a) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice, to the extent legally permitted, so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order, and (b) discloses only the portion legally required to be disclosed.

5. NO LICENSE; RETURN OF MATERIALS. No license or other right under any patent, copyright, trademark, or trade secret is granted by this Agreement or by any disclosure hereunder. Upon the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party and all copies thereof and certify such return or destruction in writing, except that one archival copy may be retained solely for compliance purposes, which shall remain subject to Section 2.

6. TERM AND SURVIVAL. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of three (3) years, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party. The obligations of the Receiving Party under Sections 2, 4, and 5 with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the term shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years thereafter; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

7. REMEDIES. Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause the Disclosing Party irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate, and agrees that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, without posting a bond, in addition to any other available remedies.

8. GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New Castle County, Delaware.

9. MISCELLANEOUS. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, written or oral, relating thereto. This Agreement may be amended only in a writing signed by both Parties, and neither Party may assign it without the other Party's prior written consent. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.
