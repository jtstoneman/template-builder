NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into and made effective as of April 12, 2023 (the "Effective Date"), by and between Pinebrook Media Group, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Discloser"), and Atlas Print Partners LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (the "Recipient"), each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

The Parties wish to explore a potential commercial printing and distribution relationship (the "Purpose"), in connection with which the Discloser may disclose confidential information to the Recipient, and therefore agree as follows:

1. Definitions. "Confidential Information" means all non-public information, in any form or medium, disclosed by or on behalf of the Discloser to the Recipient, whether before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, editorial calendars, subscriber and advertiser data, pricing, print specifications, financial projections, vendor lists, and the existence and terms of the Parties' discussions regarding the Purpose.

2. Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that the Recipient can demonstrate by written records: (a) is or becomes generally known to the public through no act or omission of the Recipient; (b) was rightfully in the Recipient's possession, free of any confidentiality obligation, prior to disclosure by the Discloser; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation owed to the Discloser; or (d) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

3. Confidentiality Obligations. The Recipient shall: (a) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose; (b) not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Discloser; (c) restrict access to the Confidential Information to those of its employees, officers, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement; and (d) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care. The Recipient shall promptly notify the Discloser in writing of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

4. Compelled Disclosure. If the Recipient is required by law or by valid order of a court or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so notwithstanding Section 3, provided that the Recipient, to the extent legally permitted, gives the Discloser prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation in seeking a protective order or other remedy, and discloses only the portion legally required to be disclosed.

5. Return or Destruction. Upon the earlier of the Discloser's written request or the expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Recipient shall promptly return to the Discloser or destroy all Confidential Information, including all copies, extracts, and summaries in any medium. If the Recipient elects destruction, an authorized officer of the Recipient shall provide the Discloser with a written certification confirming that such destruction has been completed. The Recipient may retain one archival copy solely as required by law, which copy shall remain subject to Section 3 for so long as it is retained.

6. Term and Survival. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for two (2) years thereafter, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice. The Recipient's obligations under Sections 3, 4, and 5 with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the term shall survive expiration or termination for two (2) years.

7. No License; No Obligation. Nothing in this Agreement grants the Recipient any license or other right in the Confidential Information, except the limited right to use it for the Purpose under Section 3, and nothing obligates either Party to enter into any further agreement or business relationship.

8. Remedies. The Recipient acknowledges that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause the Discloser irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate, and the Discloser shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, in addition to all other available remedies, without posting a bond.

9. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles, and the Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Delaware.

10. Miscellaneous. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous understandings, whether written or oral. It may be amended only in a writing signed by both Parties, and may not be assigned without the other Party's prior written consent. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
