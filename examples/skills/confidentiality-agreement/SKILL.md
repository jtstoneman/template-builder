# Confidentiality Agreement — negotiation playbook

Playbook for Confidentiality Agreements. Journal to date covers two clauses: term-survival (heavily evidenced, 10 matters) and non-solicitation (2 matters). All entries dated 2026-07-10 by jane@firm.com.

## Clause playbook

### non-solicitation

**Position** — Retain a non-solicitation clause with a preferred duration of 18 months. Do not drop the non-solicit where the counterparty will meet our key engineers/personnel during diligence. [j:11] [j:12]

**Fallbacks (in order):**
1. Reduce duration from 18 to 12 months in exchange for the counterparty accepting a broader general-solicitation carve-out ([j:12])

**Red lines:**
- Refused to remove the non-solicit entirely where counterparty has diligence access to our key engineers ([j:11])

### term-survival

**Position** — Survival of general confidentiality obligations capped at 5 years, measured from termination/expiration of the agreement. Trade-secret information survives indefinitely as a separate carve-out. This 5-year ceiling is firm policy tied to records retention and has held in all ten matters to date. Open at 3 years where the deal allows. [j:1] [j:2] [j:3] [j:4] [j:5] [j:6] [j:7] [j:8] [j:9] [j:10]

**Fallbacks (in order):**
1. Accept counterparty asks at or below the 5-year ceiling without negotiation (3-year matching term accepted in [j:3]; 2-year accepted where residuals are excluded in [j:7])
2. Counter any ask above 5 years down to 5 years, adding an indefinite trade-secret carve-out (countered 7->5 with carve-out [j:1]; 10->5 [j:4]; 6->5 [j:6]; indefinite->5+carve-out, accepted by counterparty [j:8]; 8->5 [j:10])
3. Concede up from our 3-year opening to the full 5 years only in exchange for a reciprocal concession (conceded to 5 for destruction certification in [j:5])

**Red lines:**
- Indefinite/open-ended survival of GENERAL confidentiality — refused; only trade secrets may survive indefinitely ([j:2])
- Never exceed 5 years for general information ([j:4], [j:10])
- Survival measured on a rolling basis from each disclosure — refused; survival must run from termination so it is measurable and auditable ([j:9])
